EnPowered
Financing Accelerator

My Role

Lead UX/UI Designer

Deliverables

UX Design, UI Design, Information Architecture

Focus

Clean Energy Financing, B2B SaaS

Overview

Clean energy financing was a tangled mess of complex models and time-consuming paperwork. EnPowered’s partners were struggling to help their customers find the best financing options, leading to frustration and lost opportunities.

As the sole UX/UI Designer, I guided the product from a simple spreadsheet to a scalable, cloud-based platform. My responsibilities included user research, interaction design, information architecture, and building the brand's design system.

The Outcome

The EnPowered Financing Accelerator became a powerful tool that streamlined the financing process. Partners could generate accurate financial estimates in minutes and directly share financing applications with their customers, significantly increasing the rate at which projects were funded.

Designing for Speed and Clarity

Our goal was clear: create a simple, scalable platform that would empower partners to become financing experts. We knew the solution had to simplify the entire process, from initial estimate to final application.

The First Iteration: Typeform Wizard

We started with a "wizard" approach using a third-party tool, Typeform. This step-by-step format was a huge improvement over the spreadsheet, as it guided users and produced a clean, automated proposal. Through user calls and usage data, we received positive feedback that validated our direction.

Second Iteration: The In-House Pivot

We received positive feedback from partners after calls with them, and received valuable insights from their using the form. We used the qualitative anecdotes and usage data to inform the next iteration of the proposal generator.

We soon learned of the limitations of using the third-party platform, as there was no easy way to adjust interest amounts, cater for depreciation, and many other factors. The product team kicked into gear to start building an in-house cloud-based solution.

Ideation & Information Architecture

I sketched out a new information architecture, moving away from the linear wizard format and explored a tab-based structure to logically group information. This challenging change provided our financing partners a holistic view of their projects (later called deals) and the ability to jump between sections without losing context.

Wireframing & Layout Experiments

Based on the previous version of the wizard form, I decided to use a left-hand tab structure to group the sections. I experimented with multiple layout configurations before settling on the simplest version.

The Evolved Solution: The Financing Accelerator

The new Financing Accelerator was a cloud-based platform with a streamlined, tab-based navigation. Partners could input project details, see a real-time summary of the financial results, and then generate a professional, branded proposal to send to their customers. We also integrated the ability for customers to complete the final financing application directly through the tool, eliminating the endless email chains and file attachments.

The Impact

While working in a startup environment meant we didn't always have a complete data set, we saw clear, qualitative evidence of its impact. Over a period of about 6 months, the Financing Accelerator allowed partners to generate accurate estimates for solar projects instantly, significantly increasing proposal adoption and empowering users to close cleaner energy deals faster.
